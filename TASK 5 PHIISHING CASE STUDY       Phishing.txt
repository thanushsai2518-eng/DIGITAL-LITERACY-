PHIISHING CASE STUDY       Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick people into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that look genuine. For example, a student may receive an email claiming to be from a bank or scholarship portal with a link. When the student clicks the link, they are taken to a fake website where they enter their details. These details are then stolen by the attacker. Students and young users are common targets because they may not verify links carefully. The consequences of phishing include financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal data. This type of cybercrime is increasing rapidly due to lack of awareness and careless online behavior.
